I can ask too many questions at times. An episode that really stands out for me was last year during the Obamacare rollout. I was going through certification to be a navigator, to help people sign up for new healthcare, which was a multi-day training covering public benefits policy, the law itself, how the insurance business worked, confidentiality and HIPAA and neutrality and so on. Illinois actually had one of the most rigorous training programs in the country- this year its model was adopted by more states.

I guess my point in all this was that I asked a lot of questions- trying to align every conceivable edge case between the income cutoffs for medicaid and the private marketplace, clarifying minutae of tax filing stati and advance premium tax credit calcluation. In retrospect, I got a couple hints from the rest of the attendees that it was becoming too much.

At my worst, my questions are just my wondering (wandering) internal monolog, externalized and with an uptalked inflection meant to elicit attention. I'm not opposed to uptalking as a rule, I should say.

With DBC I've taken to several different approaches to my endless confusion.
* Rabbit holes of googling problems- eventually I had to cut back on this because of time issues.
* Silently catalog the question- acknowledge that I don't really get it, but it seems so omnipresent and yet minor that I assume I'll internalize the new concept if I keep working.
* Keep a of record of them- For me this has been in a notebook. Even writing code down or taking minor notes by hand helps with retention. I trust that it is down somewhere and know I can prioritize it later when there is time to really formulate a question.

Even after the triage, the questions that actually need asking need to be clarified. It's easy, still, to fall into traps of not knowing what I don't know. At times I wish my teaching skills were coming in more handy here: When it comes to formulating a social research question I feel so comfortable, the utility so apparent, but with code I'm adapting to a different set of rules. Am I looking for an existing method? A general approach? Is it a simple syntax issue? At the least, I have a hierarchy of issues to check for before raising my hand.
