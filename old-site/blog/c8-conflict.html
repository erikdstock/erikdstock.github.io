Conflict in the Coop

03.8.15

2 weeks ago I rotated into a new chore with my house: Chicken care. I wanted it because I had been enjoying doing some of it anyways when it was a reason to get fresh air during the winter. It's also a quick, interesting chore where you get to check in and play with some of the funnest house animals. Twice a week I just have to empty out the poo and straw into the compost igloo (now melting- must remember to deal with that this week), refill their food and water (the least fun part), and put some new straw down. Then I play with the chickens who are usually pretty happy someone is outside in the cold with them.

So this week I got a rather upset message from a close friend and housemate about how I had been forgetting to check the nest box, and how there were several frozen and cracked eggs in it as a result. This followed immediately with a tense conversation that was pretty emblematic, I'm told, of how I deal with conflict at times.

We've all been there. You cleaned out the shit pan but not the egg box and you restrawed the shit pan but not the egg box. But I backpedaled and made excuses. I said I thought checking for eggs was a daily thing and therefore not a part of chicken care but just what everyone should do when they wake up and see no eggs on the counter, and since I hadn't seen any egg shells in the compost or on the counter lately I had assumed someone else had been checking the box. Even writing it now, it makes perfect sense.

I go through a pretty familiar pattern when pressed with small slipups like this- I minimize or, if I'm close enough with you, get defensively angry. It's not constructive and it's really hard to catch in the moment it's happening, but I do it.

I think a big part of this reaction for me is being self-conscious about forgetting something or appearing lazy. If I can just remove a complaint from what I should reasonably have to concern myself with I can neutralize it; if that doesn't work I can accuse others of picking on me.

It's a hard pattern to grow out of- even when I feel like I'm catching myself I can come off as disingenuous. For the time being I feel like I need to just keep recognizing it when it happens and to keep practicing compassion over reflex in these situations. I know, not a full answer!
